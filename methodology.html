Methodology

The data used for the visualizations comes from three main sources:

  • The Armed Conflict Location & Event Data Project (ACLED) aggregated data for Asia-Pacific , which provides detailed information on political violence and protest in all of South-East Asia, Myanmar included.
  • The World Bank, which offers a wide range of economic indicators and data on various countries.
  • The Uppsala Conflict Data Program (UCDP) Georeferenced Event Dataset (GED), which provides specific and precisely geolocated data on conflict-related events in Myanmar.
In particular:

The symbol map "A heterogeneous country" is based on the UCDP dataset where the battles considered involve the government and one of the ethnic armed groups listed in the article.

The stacked bar chart "Three decades of death" is based on the same UCDP dataset, this time considering all the events involving the government and a selected list of groups, including the main ethnic armed groups together with civilians and the NUG. The data is also cumulated by year in this step.

The waffle chart "The risk of protesting" uses the ACLED dataset to find all the events of political nature since February 2021 and the proportion of them that resulted in at least one reported death.

The choropleth map "The new battlefield" uses the ACLED dataset to visualize the geographic distribution of conflict events in Myanmar since February 2021. The data is aggregated by region and the names of the regions normalized to match the GeoJSON format.

The dumbbell chart "A country in crisis" uses the World Bank dataset to compare various economic and humanitarian indicators for Myanmar before and after the coup, with other hand selected countries. The countries where chosen based on either their population, GDP or GDP per capita.
