Methodology

The visualizations aim to explore the recent story of Myanmar, one of the least stable countries in the world listed in the top 10 crises the world can't ignore in 2026 article by the humanitarian organization International Rescue Committee. The website presents five different visualizations to analyze various aspects of Myanmar's situation, starting from the pre-existing internal conflicts between the government and the many different ethnic groups living in the country, to the military coup that took place in February 2021, and finally to the consequences on the economy and the humanitarian crisis that followed.

The data used for the visualizations comes from three main sources:

  • The Armed Conflict Location & Event Data Project (ACLED) aggregated data for Asia-Pacific , which provides detailed information on political violence and protest in all of South-East Asia, Myanmar included.
  • The World Bank, which offers a wide range of economic indicators and data on various countries.
  • The Uppsala Conflict Data Program (UCDP) Georeferenced Event Dataset (GED), which provides specific and precisely geolocated data on conflict-related events in Myanmar.
