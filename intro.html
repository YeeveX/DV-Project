Why Myanmar?

Myanmar (also known as Burma) is one of the least stable countries in the world listed in the top 10 crises the world can't ignore in 2026 article by the humanitarian organization International Rescue Committee.

In recent times, most of the world's attention has been focused on the ongoing war in Ukraine and the Middle East, but Myanmar's situation is equally dire and deserves more awareness and understanding. Myanmar is the 39th largest country in the world by land area, surpassing Afghanistan, France (considering overseas territories as well) and Ukraine, and has a population of around 51 million people, making it the 29th most populous country in the world, right above South Korea. It is a country rich in natural resources and cultural heritage, but it has been plagued by decades of internal conflict, political instability and human rights abuses.

The website presents five different visualizations to analyze various aspects of Myanmar's situation, starting from the pre-existing internal conflicts between the government and the many different ethnic groups living in the country, to the military coup that took place in February 2021, and finally to the consequences on the economy and the humanitarian crisis that followed.
