A heterogeneous country

Conflict locations between the government and the main ethnic armed groups in Myanmar from 1989 to 2023.

Myanmar is a country made of many different ethnic groups, each with its own unique culture, language, and traditions.
The central government, often led by the military, has historically marginalized many of these groups, which have organized themselves into armed factions to fight for greater autonomy or independence.

The main armed groups in Myanmar are:

  • Kachin Independence Organisation (KIO)
  • Karen National Union (KNU)
  • United League of Arakan (ULA)
  • Palaung State Liberation Front (PSLF)
  • Chin National Front (CNF)
  • Karenni National Progressive Party (KNPP)
  • Arakan Rohingya Salvation Army (ARSA)
  • Shan State Progress Party (SSPP)
  • Myanmar National Democratic Alliance Army (MNDAA)
  • Restoration Council of Shan State (RCSS)

As shown in the map, internal conflicts have been occurring throughout the entire territory of Myanmar, with different ethnic groups controlling various regions. The intensity has fluctuated over time, with some periods of relative calm followed by renewed violence.
